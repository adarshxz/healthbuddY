# Shortness of Breath (Dyspnea)

## Overview
Shortness of breath, also known as dyspnea, is the sensation of not being able to get enough air. It is a symptom that can indicate respiratory or cardiac issues and should be assessed promptly, particularly if it occurs suddenly or is severe.

## Common Causes
- **Respiratory:** Asthma, COPD, pneumonia, bronchitis, hyperventilation/anxiety, or allergies.
- **Cardiac:** Heart failure, coronary artery disease, arrhythmias.
- **Other:** Severe anemia, panic attacks, high altitudes, physical deconditioning.

## Home Care & Immediate Actions
- **For Known Conditions (e.g., Asthma or COPD):** Use your prescribed rescue inhaler (such as albuterol/salbutamol) as directed by your doctor.
- **Positioning:** Sit upright, lean forward slightly, and support yourself on a table or your knees. This helps expand the lungs.
- **Pursed-lip Breathing:** Breathe in slowly through your nose for two counts, then purse your lips (as if to blow out a candle) and breathe out slowly for four counts.
- **Stay Calm:** Anxiety can worsen breathing difficulties. Focus on slow, controlled breaths.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Call emergency services immediately if shortness of breath:
- Occurs suddenly, is severe, or worsens rapidly.
- Is accompanied by chest pain, pressure, or tightness.
- Is accompanied by a blue or gray color on your lips, face, or fingernails (cyanosis).
- Occurs with confusion, lightheadedness, or fainting.
- Is accompanied by a high fever, cough, and chills (signs of severe pneumonia).
- Prevents you from speaking in full sentences or walking even a few steps.
