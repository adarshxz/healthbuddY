# Chest Pain and Cardiac Warning Signs

## Overview
Chest pain should always be taken seriously as it can be a symptom of a life-threatening condition, such as a heart attack or pulmonary embolism. While it can also be caused by less severe issues like acid reflux, muscle strain, or anxiety, it requires careful triage.

## Symptoms of a Heart Attack
Cardiac chest pain may include:
- Pressure, fullness, squeezing, or pain in the center of the chest.
- Pain that radiates to the shoulder, arm, back, neck, jaw, or teeth.
- Pain that lasts more than a few minutes or goes away and returns.
- Shortness of breath, heavy sweating (diaphoresis), dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting.
- Nausea, indigestion, or abdominal pain (more common in women).

## Other Causes of Chest Pain
- **Gastrointestinal:** Acid reflux, heartburn, or swallowing disorders (typically worse after eating or when lying down).
- **Musculoskeletal:** Muscle strain, costochondritis (inflammation of rib cartilage), worsened by pushing on the chest wall.
- **Respiratory:** Pleurisy (inflammation of the lung lining), worsened by breathing in deeply or coughing.
- **Anxiety/Panic Attack:** Rapid breathing, chest tightness, palpitations, and intense fear.

## Home Care & Immediate Actions
- **For Non-Urgent, Known Causes:** If diagnosed with acid reflux, take prescribed antacids. If diagnosed with muscle strain, rest and apply warm compresses.
- **If Heart Attack is Suspected:**
  - DO NOT wait or attempt to treat at home.
  - Sit down, stay calm, and immediately call emergency services (911/112).
  - If recommended by emergency dispatch and you are not allergic, chew a standard adult aspirin (325 mg) or 2-4 low-dose aspirins.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Call emergency services immediately if chest pain:
- Is sudden, crushing, squeezing, or feels like a heavy weight on your chest.
- Radiates to your left arm, neck, jaw, or back.
- Is accompanied by shortness of breath, dizziness, sweating, or nausea.
- Does not subside after 5 minutes of rest or after taking prescribed nitroglycerin.
