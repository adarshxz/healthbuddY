# Cough and Common Cold

## Overview
The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. A cough is a natural reflex to clear irritants and mucus from the airways. Most colds clear up within 7 to 10 days, though a cough may linger for up to 3 weeks.

## Symptoms
Symptoms typically peak within 2 to 3 days and include:
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Sore throat and coughing
- Sneezing
- Mild body aches or headache
- Low-grade fever (especially in children)
- Post-nasal drip

## Home Care & Remedies
- **Hydration:** Drink plenty of warm liquids like herbal tea, warm water with honey and lemon, or broth to soothe the throat. Do not give honey to children under 1 year of age.
- **Rest:** Ensure adequate rest to support the immune system.
- **Humidity:** Use a cool-mist humidifier or take a steamy shower to help loosen mucus.
- **Saline Rinses:** Use saline nasal drops or sprays to relieve nasal congestion.
- **Sore Throat:** Gargle with warm salt water (1/2 teaspoon of salt in a glass of warm water).
- **Medications:** Decongestants, throat lozenges, or pain relievers (paracetamol/ibuprofen) can help relieve symptoms but do not cure the cold.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Consult a doctor or seek urgent medical care if you experience:
- Difficulty breathing, wheezing, or short breath
- Chest pain or pressure
- Coughing up blood or rust-colored mucus
- Symptoms that worsen significantly after starting to improve
- High fever that lasts more than 3 days
- Sore throat so severe that you cannot swallow saliva
