# Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

## Overview
Hypertension, or high blood pressure, occurs when the force of blood pushing against artery walls is consistently too high. It is often called the "silent killer" because it typically presents no symptoms but can lead to severe cardiovascular events like heart attacks and strokes if left unmanaged.

## Classifications
Blood pressure readings are measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and classified as:
- **Normal:** Systolic < 120 and Diastolic < 80 mmHg.
- **Elevated:** Systolic 120–129 and Diastolic < 80 mmHg.
- **Stage 1 Hypertension:** Systolic 130–139 or Diastolic 80–89 mmHg.
- **Stage 2 Hypertension:** Systolic 140+ or Diastolic 90+ mmHg.
- **Hypertensive Crisis:** Systolic > 180 and/or Diastolic > 120 mmHg (requires immediate medical attention).

## Home Care & Management
- **Regular Monitoring:** Track your blood pressure at home using a validated cuff and keep a log.
- **Dietary Choices (DASH Diet):** Limit sodium intake to under 1,500–2,000 mg per day. Consume foods rich in potassium, calcium, and magnesium (vegetables, fruits, whole grains).
- **Exercise:** Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise (e.g., brisk walking) per week.
- **Stress Management:** Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
- **Lifestyle Modifications:** Limit alcohol intake, avoid tobacco products, and maintain a healthy weight.
- **Medication Adherence:** Take prescribed blood pressure medications exactly as directed by your doctor. Do not stop taking them even if you feel fine.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Seek emergency medical attention immediately if a blood pressure reading exceeds 180/120 mmHg AND is accompanied by:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Shortness of breath
- Severe, sudden headache
- Numbness, weakness, difficulty speaking, or changes in vision (stroke signs)
- Back pain
- Seizures or confusion
