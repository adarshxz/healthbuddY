# Diarrhea and Vomiting (Gastroenteritis)

## Overview
Diarrhea and vomiting are common symptoms usually caused by viral or bacterial gastroenteritis (often called stomach flu or food poisoning). The primary hazard associated with persistent vomiting and diarrhea is dehydration, especially in children and the elderly.

## Symptoms
- Loose, watery stools (diarrhea)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Stomach cramps and low-grade fever
- Signs of mild-to-moderate dehydration: dry mouth, thirst, reduced urine output, dark-colored urine, dizziness, or headache.

## Home Care & Remedies
- **Rehydration:** This is the most crucial step. Sip small amounts of fluids frequently. Use Oral Rehydration Solutions (ORS) or diluted sports drinks. Avoid drinking too much at once, as this can trigger further vomiting.
- **Dietary Adjustments:** Once vomiting has ceased for several hours, introduce bland foods slowly (BRAT diet: bananas, rice, applesauce, toast). Avoid dairy, high-fat, spicy, or sugary foods, caffeine, and alcohol.
- **Probiotics:** Probiotics can help restore balance to the gut microbiome and shorten the duration of diarrhea.
- **Medication:** Do not use anti-diarrheal medications (like loperamide) if you have a high fever or bloody stools, as they can prolong the infection. Consult a healthcare provider first.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Consult a doctor or seek immediate medical care if you experience:
- Severe dehydration (inability to keep liquids down for 24 hours, extreme thirst, little or no urination, confusion, sunken eyes, rapid heart rate).
- High fever (above 38.9°C or 102°F).
- Severe abdominal pain or cramps.
- Stools that are bloody, black, or contain pus.
- Vomit that is bloody or green/yellow (bilious).
- Symptoms that do not improve after 2 days (for adults) or 24 hours (for children).
