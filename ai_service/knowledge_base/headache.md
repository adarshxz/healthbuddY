# Headache and Migraine

## Overview
Headaches are very common and usually not a sign of a serious illness. The most common types are tension headaches, migraines, and cluster headaches. Understanding trigger factors and practicing self-care can help manage them.

## Symptoms
- **Tension Headache:** A constant, dull ache affecting both sides of the head, often described as a tight band around the forehead.
- **Migraine:** Moderate to severe throbbing pain, usually on one side of the head. Often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and extreme sensitivity to light and sound.
- **Cluster Headache:** Severe, sharp, or piercing pain around one eye, occurring in cycles (clusters).

## Home Care & Remedies
- **Rest in a Quiet, Dark Room:** This is highly effective for migraines.
- **Apply a Compress:** Use a cold compress or ice pack on the forehead/temples for migraines, or a warm compress on the neck/shoulders for tension headaches.
- **Stay Hydrated:** Drink a large glass of water, as dehydration is a common headache trigger.
- **Manage Stress:** Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or gentle stretching.
- **Avoid Known Triggers:** Track foods, stress, sleep patterns, or environmental factors that seem to precede headaches.
- **Medications:** Pain relievers like paracetamol, ibuprofen, or aspirin may help. For migraines, specific prescription medications (triptans) may be recommended by a doctor.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Seek emergency medical attention if a headache:
- Is sudden, severe, and reaches maximum intensity within seconds (often called a "thunderclap" headache).
- Is accompanied by fever, stiff neck, confusion, seizures, or double vision.
- Follows a recent head injury.
- Is accompanied by numbness, weakness, difficulty speaking, or loss of balance (stroke warning signs).
- Is new or different and occurs if you are over 50 years old or have a history of cancer.
