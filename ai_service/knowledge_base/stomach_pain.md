# Stomach Pain (Abdominal Pain)

## Overview
Abdominal pain can range from mild indigestion or gas to serious conditions like appendicitis, gallstones, or bowel obstruction. The location and severity of the pain are crucial factors in determining the cause and urgency of care.

## Symptoms and Locations
- **Upper Abdomen:** Often related to reflux, gastritis, or gallstones (right side).
- **Lower Right Abdomen:** A key indicator of appendicitis if the pain starts around the navel and moves to the lower right.
- **Lower Left Abdomen:** Frequently associated with diverticulitis.
- **General/Diffuse Pain:** Often caused by gas, bloating, indigestion, or viral gastroenteritis (stomach flu).

## Home Care & Remedies
For mild abdominal pain (e.g., indigestion, gas, or mild cramps):
- Sip water or clear fluids. Avoid solid foods for a few hours.
- Eat small, bland meals (crackers, toast, rice, applesauce) once appetite returns.
- Avoid dairy, caffeine, alcohol, carbonated drinks, and fatty or spicy foods.
- Place a warm hot water bottle or heating pad on the abdomen.
- Walk gently to help pass trapped gas.
- Refrain from taking NSAIDs (like ibuprofen or aspirin) without medical advice, as they can irritate the stomach lining.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Seek immediate emergency medical care if stomach pain is:
- Severe, sudden, or unbearable.
- Accompanied by high fever, chills, or cold sweats.
- Accompanied by persistent vomiting and inability to keep fluids down.
- Accompanied by blood in vomit (looks like coffee grounds) or blood in stools (black, tarry, or bright red).
- Accompanied by a rigid, hard, or extremely tender abdomen when touched.
- Present during pregnancy.
- Accompanied by yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice).
