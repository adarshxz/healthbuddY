# Skin Rash and Allergic Reactions

## Overview
A skin rash is a noticeable change in the texture or color of your skin. It can be caused by contact allergies, infections, autoimmune conditions, medications, or system-wide allergic reactions (anaphylaxis).

## Common Types
- **Contact Dermatitis:** Red, itchy rash caused by direct contact with an irritant or allergen (e.g., poison ivy, soaps, cosmetics).
- **Hives (Urticaria):** Raised, red, extremely itchy welts that can appear and disappear quickly, often triggered by food, medication, or stress.
- **Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis):** Dry, red, itchy patches of skin that are chronic and flare up periodically.
- **Infectious Rash:** Rashes caused by viruses (measles, chickenpox), bacteria, or fungi (ringworm).

## Home Care & Remedies
For mild, localized rashes:
- Avoid scratching to prevent skin damage and secondary bacterial infections.
- Apply a cool, damp compress to the area to soothe itching and inflammation.
- Take a lukewarm bath (colloidal oatmeal baths can be highly soothing).
- Apply mild over-the-counter creams, such as 1% hydrocortisone cream or calamine lotion.
- For hives or allergic contact dermatitis, oral over-the-counter antihistamines (like cetirizine or loratadine) can reduce itching and swelling.
- Wear loose-fitting, soft cotton clothing. Avoid harsh soaps or scented products.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Seek emergency medical attention immediately if a rash:
- Is accompanied by difficulty breathing, swallowing, or swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat (signs of a life-threatening anaphylactic reaction).
- Spreads rapidly across the body within hours.
- Starts to blister, open up, or becomes extremely painful.
- Is accompanied by a high fever, joint pain, or sore throat.
- Shows signs of infection (pus, warmth, red streaks radiating from the rash, or swelling).
