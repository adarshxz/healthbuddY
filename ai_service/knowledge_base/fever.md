# Fever (High Temperature)

## Overview
A fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often due to an infection or illness. It is a sign that your body's immune system is actively fighting off pathogens. For adults, a temperature above 37.8°C (100°F) is generally considered a fever.

## Symptoms
Common symptoms associated with a fever include:
- High body temperature (monitored with a thermometer)
- Shivering, shaking chills, and sweating
- Headaches and muscle aches
- Loss of appetite
- Mild dehydration
- Feeling weak, fatigued, or lethargic

## Home Care & Remedies
If you have a mild fever (under 38.9°C or 102°F) and feel reasonably comfortable:
- Drink plenty of fluids (water, clear broths, oral rehydration solutions) to prevent dehydration.
- Get plenty of rest to help your body recover.
- Wear lightweight, breathable clothing and use a light blanket.
- Keep the room temperature comfortable.
- If needed, take over-the-counter fever reducers such as paracetamol (acetaminophen) or ibuprofen. Always follow packaging instructions and avoid aspirin for children.

## Red Flags & Emergency
Seek immediate medical attention or call emergency services if the fever is accompanied by:
- Severe headache or stiff neck
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Confusion, extreme drowsiness, or trouble waking up
- A new, unexplained rash that spreads quickly
- Seizures or convulsions
- Persistent vomiting and inability to keep fluids down
- Fever above 40°C (104°F) that does not respond to medication
